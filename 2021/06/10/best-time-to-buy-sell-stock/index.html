You are given an integer array prices where prices[i] is the price of a given stock on the ith day, and an integer k.

Find the maximum profit you can achieve. You may complete at most k transactions.

Note: You may not engage in multiple transactions simultaneously (i.e., you must sell the stock before you buy again).

The problem above is one of the variants of buy & sell stock, which may be solved in different ways according the maximum number of transactions k. However, in this post, I wanna try a comman solution to this puzzle.

Analysis

Here I use hold1[i] & hold0[i] to represent the best profits we can achieve on the i-th day when we hold 1 or 0 stock respectively , since we cannot engage in multiple transactions simultaneously, which means we can hold at most 1 stock on the i-th day. If we don’t hold stock on the i-th day, we may sold it on the (i-1)-th day or we just didn’t hold it since (i-1)-th day. Similarly, if we hold 1 stock on the i-th day, we may just buy it or bought it yesterday. And don’t forget, we always want the best profit:

$$
hold1[i] = max(hold1[i-1], hold0[i-1]-prices[i]),\\
hold0[i] = max(hold0[i-1], hold1[i-1]+prices[i])
$$

where we always have

$$
hold1[0] = -prices[0],\\
hold0[0] = 0.
$$

When there is a constraint of limit of transactions, things become a little different, we need to track the transaction. And the 1-d array cannot do it as the dimension follows the day. So we require another dimension to keep the number of transactions: hold1[i][k] can tell us the best profit when we hold 1 stock for the k-th time on the i-th day. So here, we come to
$$
hold1[i][k] = max(hold1[i-1][k], hold0[i-1][k-1]-prices[i]),\\
hold0[i][k] = max(hold0[i-1][k], hold1[i-1][k]+prices[i])
$$

obviously, for any k,
$$
hold1[0][k] = -prices[0],\\
hold0[0][k] = 0
$$
, when k varies at [0, K).

Also for any i, we can obtain
$$
hold0[i][-1] = 0
$$
Since we have never buy a stock.

K = 1

When we can commit one transaction，hold1&hold0 will degenerate into 1-d array，since k is just 0。
$$
hold1[i] = max(hold1[i-1], -prices[i]),\\
hold0[i] = max(hold0[i-1], hold1[i-1]+prices[i]).
$$
For space saving we can use just two variables:
$$
hold1, hold0 \leftarrow max(hold, -price[i]), max(hold0, hold+prices[i])
$$

Cooldown

ou are given an array prices where prices[i] is the price of a given stock on the ith day.

Find the maximum profit you can achieve. You may complete as many transactions as you like (i.e., buy one and sell one share of the stock multiple times) with the following restrictions:

After you sell your stock, you cannot buy stock on the next day (i.e., cooldown one day).
Note: You may not engage in multiple transactions simultaneously (i.e., you must sell the stock before you buy again).

Here, I would like hold0[i][0] to represent that we don’t have stock and we’re not able to buy a stock(since we just sold it yesterday), and hold0[i][1] to represent that we don’t have a stock but we can buy one. And then the formula comes to:
$$
hold1[i] = max(hold1[i-1], hold0[i-1][1]-prices[i]),\\
hold0[i][0] = hold1[i-1]+prices[i], \\
hold0[i][1] = max(hold0[i-1][0], hold0[i-1][1]).
$$
